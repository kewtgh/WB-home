How We Work

We do not separate recruitment from organizational reality. Strong hiring decisions depend on clear business objectives, accurate role design, and systems that support execution after the hire is made.

Step 01

Clarify Business Objectives

We begin by understanding the company’s stage, strategy, operating priorities, and leadership intent.

Step 02

Define Organization and Role

We sharpen structure, role expectations, team interfaces, and hiring criteria before execution begins.

Step 03

Assess the Market

We use market mapping, business analysis, and target talent research where needed to improve accuracy.

Step 04

Execute with Structured Process

From search to assessment, offer coordination, and post-hire stability, we emphasize discipline and quality control.

Step 05

Strengthen the System Behind the People

For broader needs, we extend support into OKR, SOP, training systems, and management capability-building.

Case Studies

Case A

Growth-Stage Technology Platform

We supported a scaling technology company across leadership hiring, role design, team structure, and operating alignment so growth would not outpace execution capacity.

Detail

Leadership Hiring and Operating Alignment

The project began with founder priorities, product-stage needs, and organization bottlenecks, then moved into critical hiring, decision rights, and execution mechanisms for a faster-scaling team.

  • Phase 1: clarify growth priorities, org gaps, leadership accountabilities, and key interface points.
  • Phase 2: hire critical product, commercial, and operations leaders against explicit role scorecards.
  • Phase 3: strengthen OKR rhythm, SOP clarity, and cross-team execution discipline during scale-up.
Case B

Teacher Development & Training Design

We designed a faculty-development program that combined internal top teachers with external speakers to strengthen mission, commitment, and professional growth.

Detail

Dual-Speaker Training Format

The program focused less on abstract methodology and more on real teaching experience, personal motivation, practical tension, and long-term development.

  • Format: pair internal outstanding teachers with external facilitators for complementary perspectives.
  • Content: structure sessions around stories, choices, setbacks, reflection, and educational conviction.
  • Outcome: build a more stable teacher-development atmosphere and stronger values alignment.
Case C

Critical-Role Hiring Judgment System

We built a hiring decision framework for critical roles, covering role mission, outcomes, capability, values alignment, motivation, and expectation consistency.

Detail

From Candidate Evaluation to Organizational Decision Capability

Hiring mistakes often come from inconsistent standards rather than weak interviewing. Role definitions remain unclear, values are not operationalized, and competence, motivation, and fit are judged without a shared framework.

  • Define role mission, expected outcomes, and required capabilities upfront.
  • Assess competence, cultural alignment, motivation, and expectation consistency as separate dimensions.
  • Use structured interviews, calibration, and hiring review to reduce mismatch and early attrition.
